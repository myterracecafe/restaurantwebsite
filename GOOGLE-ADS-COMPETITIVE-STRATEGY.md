## 1. EXECUTIVE TAKE

We are not entering a crowded auction blind — we are entering one where the strong players are few and beatable on specific, nameable seams, and the weak players are invisible online. The job is to monetize that asymmetry fast.

### The 5 biggest competitor weaknesses we exploit

1. **The proof gap (our cheapest, biggest weapon).** Only one direct rooftop rival — Seven Hills (Google 4.5★, ~15,870 reviews) — out-reviews us, and it is widely flagged as overpriced/inconsistent ("tourist trap," "supermarket breakfast"). Every other Tier-1/Tier-2 rooftop (Best View ~144, Skyline ~224, Roofstar ~218, Sultanahmet Rooftop ~35, Queb on Facebook proof) shows a **fraction** of our 4.7★ / 5,600+ Google reviews. Almost none of them surface review counts in ads. We put **"4.7★ · 5,600+ reviews · Travelers' Choice 2025"** in headlines and callouts on day one. Nobody nearby can copy that this quarter.

2. **The sea-panorama gap.** Every rival leads with "Hagia Sophia + Blue Mosque, side by side." Our Cankurtaran/Küçük Ayasofya position lets us own a claim **none of them can truthfully make: Hagia Sophia + the Marmara Sea in one frame.** "Restaurant with sea view Sultanahmet," "Marmara view dinner," "sunset over the sea" are ours to take cheaply.

3. **The localized-language gap (the cheapest reservation volume on the table).** Seven Hills is genuinely multilingual; almost everyone else is EN/TR only. Competitors *publish* AR/DE/RU content but rarely run **tight, localized Search ads** on terms like `مطعم بإطلالة على ايا صوفيا`, `restaurant mit blick auf hagia sophia`, `ресторан с видом на айя софию`. Our 7-language site (TR/EN/AR/DE/RU/ES/FR) lets us serve the exact Gulf/German/Russian intent these rivals leave on the table. **(VERIFY thin paid coverage in Transparency Center.)**

4. **The cruise-intent leak we recapture.** GetYourGuide/Viator hijack "Istanbul sunset dinner / dinner with view" with Bosphorus **boat cruises** — a product mismatch for someone who wants to sit and eat. Counter-copy — *"No boat needed — stay on land, watch the sun set over Hagia Sophia and the sea"* — converts the misrouted intent into a reservation.

5. **The owned-site / Quality-Score gap among local rivals.** Roofstar, Skyline, Old İstanbul, Best View, Massa, Ottomans Kitchen live almost entirely on Tripadvisor/Quandoo/Wheree with thin or no owned sites — "ranking ghosts." A real multilingual landing site (clear CTA, hours, location, proof, FAQ) gives us **better Ad Rank at lower CPC** than rivals who'd route paid clicks to thin listing pages. They are not paid-search threats; they are share we take by simply showing up.

### Our unfair advantages

- **One venue covers every money cluster.** Queb owns breakfast-content, Best View owns steak, Roofstar owns hookah — **no rival combines serpme Turkish breakfast + charcoal mangal/kebab + seafood + nargile + sunset under 5,600+ reviews.** One ad group per cluster, headline always matching the query, all backed by the same proof line.
- **The 08:30 open = a breakfast money-query that's wide open.** "Turkish breakfast with view" is contested only by Seven Hills + Queb. We daypart hard into 07:00–11:00 and own a high-margin, lower-competition window.
- **WhatsApp instant booking is a friction-killer, not a weakness.** OTAs force accounts/fees; many restaurants bury booking. "Reserve in 30 seconds on WhatsApp — a human replies" is a conversion advantage for tourists, *provided* we never imply online checkout (prices are hidden, no online ordering).

### The one honest caveat that gates everything
Every Tier-1/2 threat with real digital chops (Queb, Sultanahmet Rooftop, Roofstar, Best View) has **online booking / Reserve-with-Google**; we have WhatsApp/phone only and hidden menu prices. This costs us ad extensions, conversion smoothness, and some Quality Score. **Add a one-tap WhatsApp/"Reserve" path + structured data (Restaurant schema, hours, geo) before or alongside launch.** This is the single highest-leverage non-bidding task.

## 5. UNIT ECONOMICS

**All TRY. Reservation = primary conversion (WhatsApp/phone click). Flagged assumptions replace with owner's real POS + WhatsApp-to-seated ratio when available.**

**Inputs:** avg spend **₺350/guest** (owner-confirmed); avg party size **2.7 (EST**, conservative — destination rooftops often run 3.0+); contribution margin **65% (EST** — food/bev/hookah/breakfast are high-margin sit-down).

- **Revenue / reservation = 350 × 2.7 = ₺945**
- **Contribution / reservation = 945 × 0.65 = ₺614**

| Party size | Revenue / reservation |
|---|---|
| 2.5 (couple-heavy, conservative) | ₺875 |
| **2.7 (planning base)** | **₺945** |
| 3.0 (group-heavy, optimistic) | ₺1,050 |

**Break-even & ROAS:**
- **Break-even CPA (on margin) = ₺614** → zero profit on ad-driven covers at this CPA.
- **Break-even ROAS = 1 ÷ 0.65 = 1.54x** (must generate ≥₺1.54 revenue per ₺1 spend to cover COGS + ad cost).

**Target CPA — decisive:**

| Tier | CPA | Logic | ROAS (rev/spend) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Healthy target (steady-state goal)** | **₺150** | ~16% of contribution; leaves ~₺464/cover | **6.3x** |
| Mid | ₺200 | Reasonable scaling band | 4.7x |
| **Hard ceiling (do-not-exceed)** | **₺300** | ~32% of contribution, ~6.2% of revenue; above this, scale back/pause | 3.15x |
| Learning (first 2–3 wks only) | ₺350–400 | Tolerated while Smart Bidding gathers data, then tighten | ~2.4–2.7x |

> **Decision rule:** **₺150 healthy / ₺300 hard ceiling.** Under ₺150 = scale spend. Sustained above ₺300 = fix targeting before adding budget.
